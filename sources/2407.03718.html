Multi-Convformer: Extending Conformer with
Multiple Convolution Kernels

Abstract

Convolutions have become essential in state-of-the-art end-to-end Automatic Speech RecognitionÂ (ASR) systems due to their efficient modelling of local context. Notably, its use in Conformers has led to superior performance compared to vanilla Transformer-based ASR systems. While components other than the convolution module in the Conformer have been reexamined, altering the convolution module itself has been far less explored. Towards this, we introduce Multi-ConvformerÂ  that uses multiple convolution kernels within the convolution module of the Conformer in conjunction with gating. This helps in improved modeling of local dependencies at varying granularities. Our model rivals existing Conformer variants such as CgMLP and E-Branchformer in performance, while being more parameter efficient. We empirically compare our approach with Conformer and its variants across four different datasets and three different modelling paradigms and show up to 8% relative word error rateÂ (WER) improvements.

1 Introduction

In recent years, end-to-end automatic speech recognition (ASR) systems have emerged as the preferred model of choice to obtain state-of-the-art ASR performance. An integral component of such systems is the speech encoderÂ [1, 2] that uses Attention via TransformersÂ [3] to map the input speech into high-level acoustic representations. Transformer-based ASR systemsÂ [4, 5] often struggle to model local relationships effectively. To address this limitation, Gulati etÂ al.Â [6] proposed the ConformerÂ [7] architecture, that combines multi-headed attention with convolutionsÂ [8, 9, 10]. With the advent of Conformer models, the idea of using convolutions alongside attention to independently model both local and global relationships has been widely exploredÂ [11, 12, 13, 14].

Despite these notable advancements, prior workÂ [11] has shown that the use of fixed-kernel convolutions within these models creates a bottleneck, forcing the model to re-purpose some of its attention heads to function as local information extractors. This negatively impacts the performance of attention, whose primary purpose is to model global information. To address this limitation, in our work, we propose a multiple convolution-based enhancement to the Conformer architecture (that we call Multi-Convformer). Additionally, we incorporate gating, a technique that has proven to be effective in encoder architecturesÂ [15, 11, 12]. By combining these approaches, we achieve significant improvementsÂ (up to 8% relative WER improvement) over the original Conformer architecture and perform at par or better than its variants such as CgMLPÂ [15] and E-BranchformerÂ [12]. The use of multiple convolutions in order to generate better local context has been widely adopted in image-related tasksÂ [16, 17, 18, 19, 20]. Such enhancements have also been used in speech emotion recognitionÂ [21] and robust ASRÂ [22, 23]; the latter works use multiple convolutions within fully convolutional or TDNN-style architectures (unlike our work).
